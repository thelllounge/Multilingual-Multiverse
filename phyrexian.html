What is Phyrexian?

The Phyrexian language is a language from New Phyrexia. It was originally featured in ads for the Scars of Mirrodin block and has since then been used in many other places as well. It is written along a central glyph and is written vertically. Despite the vertical writing in text there are quite a few real life examples of horizontal writing samples. These exist mainly as alternate language promo cards.

The first use of Phyrexian on a card was with a judge promo for Elesh Norn in 2014. This card makes it very clear that Phyrexian is actually a conlang instead of a cypher as the numbers in the text are in very different locations from the English version.

While Elesh Norn was the first Phyrexian praetor to get a Phyrexian text treatment she wasn't the only one. Wizards of the Coast has also release all of the original praetor cards with the treatment as well in a Secret Lair.

Vorinclex has the special honor of being the only character that has two different cards in the Phyrexian langauge. The first one was printed in the Kaldheim set as an alternate version of the card.

The final card at this moment in time that has been printed in the Phyrexian language is a basic swamp.

Card text isn't the only place that the Phyrexian langauge has been used on Magic cards, though. We can also see some in a few different card arts for Phyrexian related cards. For example, Phyrexian Scriptures has flavor text from Dark Ritual carved into the side of what appears to be an altar. The Phyrexian Scriptures art also references the art from the Urza's Saga version of Dark Ritual (which contains the afforementioned flavor text).

The Ultimate Masters version of Phyrexian Altar also contains an altar with Phyrexian script carved into it.
